About Credit Repair

What exactly is credit repair?
Credit repair is the process of disputing inaccurate, incomplete, or unverifiable information on your credit reports. Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), you have the legal right to challenge any information that doesn't meet federal reporting standards. We analyze your reports for Metro 2 format violations and FCRA errors, then create custom dispute letters to get those items removed.
Is credit repair legal?
Absolutely. The Fair Credit Reporting Act gives every consumer the right to dispute inaccurate information. Credit repair organizations are regulated under the Credit Repair Organizations Act (CROA), which we fully comply with. We never ask you to misrepresent informationâ€”we dispute items based on legitimate reporting errors and violations.
What types of items can be removed?
Any item that contains inaccurate information, cannot be verified, or violates Metro 2 reporting standards can potentially be removed. This includes collections, charge-offs, late payments, repossessions, foreclosures, bankruptcies (if reporting errors exist), and inquiries. Even legitimate debts can be removed if they're being reported incorrectly.
How long does credit repair take?
Every situation is different. By law, credit bureaus have 30-45 days to investigate disputes. Some clients see results in the first round (30 days), others need multiple rounds. Our 6-month coverage ensures we have time to work through stubborn items. Many clients see significant improvement within 60-90 days.

Why do I mail the letters myself?
Disputes sent directly from you (the consumer) carry more legal weight than those sent by third parties. Credit bureaus are legally required to investigate disputes from consumers. When you mail the letters yourself with certified mail, you create a paper trail and ensure the bureaus take your disputes seriously.
